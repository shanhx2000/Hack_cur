dip·loid 
/ ˈdipˌloid/  Genetics 
•
adj.
(of a cell or nucleus) containing two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent. Compare with haploid.
 ∎ 
(of an organism or part) composed of diploid cells.


•
n.
a diploid cell, organism, or species.

DERIVATIVES: 
dip·loi·dy
/ -ˌloidē/ n.
