[
fell1 
/ fel/
•
past of fall.

fell2 
•
v. [tr.]
(usu. be felled)
cut down (a tree).
 ∎ 
knock down:
strong winds felled power lines fig. 
corruption that felled the financial system in Thailand.


•
n.
an amount of timber cut.

fell3 
•
n.
a hill or stretch of high moorland, esp. in northern England.
an area of fell and moor.

fell4 
•
adj.  poetic/lit. 
of terrible evil or ferocity; deadly:
sorcerers use spells to achieve their fell ends.

PHRASES: 
in
one fell swoop
all at one time:
nothing can topple the government in one fell swoop.

fell5 
•
n.  archaic 
an animal's hide or skin with its hair.
