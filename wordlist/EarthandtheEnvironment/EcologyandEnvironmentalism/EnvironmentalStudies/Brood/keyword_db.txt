brood 
/ broōd/
•
n.
a family of young animals, esp. of a bird, produced at one hatching or birth:
a brood of chicks.
 ∎ 
bee or wasp larvae.
 ∎ inf. 
all of the children in a family:
she was brought up by a loving stepfather as part of a brood of eight.
 ∎ 
a group of things or people having a similar character:
a remarkable brood of writers.


•
v.
1. [intr.]
think deeply about something that makes one unhappy:
he brooded over his need to find a wife.
2.
[tr.]
(of a bird) sit on (eggs) to hatch them.
 ∎ 
(of a fish, frog, or invertebrate) hold (developing eggs) within the body.


3. [usu. foll. by over]
(of silence, a storm, etc.) hang or hover closely:
a winter storm broods over the lake.


•
adj.
(of an animal) kept to be used for breeding:
a brood mare.
