cap·sule 
/ ˈkapsəl; ˈkapˌsoōl/
•
n.
a small case or container, esp. a round or cylindrical one.
 ∎ 
a small, soluble case of gelatin containing a dose of medicine, swallowed whole.
 ∎ 
a top or cover for a bottle, esp. the foil or plastic covering the cork of a wine bottle.
 ∎ 
short for space capsule.
 ∎  [as  adj.]  fig. 
(of a piece of writing) shortened but retaining the essence of the original:
a capsule review of the movie.
 ∎ 
 Anat. 
a tough sheath or membrane that encloses something in the body, such as a kidney, a lens, or a synovial joint.
 ∎ 
 Biol. 
a gelatinous layer forming the outer surface of some bacterial cells.
 ∎ 
 Bot. 
a dry fruit that releases its seeds by bursting open when ripe, such as a pea pod.
 ∎ 
 Bot. 
the spore-producing structure of mosses and liverworts, typically borne on a stalk.
