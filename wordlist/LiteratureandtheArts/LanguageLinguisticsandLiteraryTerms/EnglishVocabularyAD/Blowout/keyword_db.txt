[
blow·out 
/ ˈblōˌout/
•
n.
1.
a sudden rupture or malfunction of a part or apparatus due to pressure, in particular:
 ∎ 
a bursting of an automobile tire.
 ∎ fig. 
an outburst of anger; an argument:
that exchange led to a big blowout five years ago.
 ∎ 
an uprush of oil or gas from a well.
 ∎ 
 inf. 
a melting of an electric fuse.

2. inf. 
an easy victory in a sporting contest or an election:
they had lost seven games—four by blowouts.

3. inf. 
a large or lavish meal or social gathering.

4.
a hollow eroded by the wind.


•
adj.
huge; all-consuming:
 year-end blowout sale.
