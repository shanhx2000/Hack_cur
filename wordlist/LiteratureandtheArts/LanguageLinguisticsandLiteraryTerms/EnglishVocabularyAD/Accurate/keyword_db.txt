ac·cu·rate 
/ ˈakyərit/
•
adj.
1.
(of information, measurements, statistics, etc.) correct in all details; exact.
 ∎ 
(of an instrument or method) capable of giving such information.
 ∎ 
(of a piece of work) meticulously careful and free from errors.
 ∎ 
faithfully or fairly representing the truth about someone or something:
an accurate likeness of Mozart.
2.
(of a weapon or the person using it) capable of reaching the intended target.
 ∎ 
(of a shot or throw, or the person making it) successful in reaching a target.



DERIVATIVES: 
ac·cu·rate·ly
adv.
