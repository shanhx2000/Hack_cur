[
bu·reau 
/ ˈbyoŏrō/
•
n.
(pl.  bu·reaus
or bu·reaux
/ ˈbyoŏrōz/ )
1.
a chest of drawers.
2.
(abbr.: bur.)
an office or department for transacting particular business:
a news bureau.
 ∎ 
the office in a particular place of an organization based elsewhere:
the London bureau of the Washington Post.
 ∎ 
a government department:
the intelligence bureau.



ORIGIN: late 17th cent.: from French, originally
‘baize’ (used to cover writing desks), from Old French burel, probably from bure ‘dark brown,’.
