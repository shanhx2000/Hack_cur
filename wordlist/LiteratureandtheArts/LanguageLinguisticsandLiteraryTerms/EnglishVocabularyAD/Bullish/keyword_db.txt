[
bull·ish 
/ ˈboŏlish/
•
adj.
1.
resembling a bull:
a sketch of his round, bullish head.
 ∎ 
stupid or oafish; bullheaded:
it's impossible to reason with such a bullish man.
 ∎ 
assertively masculine; macho:
surrounded by girls and the aura of bullish manhood.

2.
 Stock Market 
characterized by rising share prices:
the market was bullish.
 ∎ 
(of a dealer) inclined to buy because of an anticipated rise in prices.


3.
feeling especially hopeful; optimistic:
challenging those who are bullish on the nation's economic prospects.


DERIVATIVES: 
bull·ish·ly
adv.
bull·ish·ness
n.
