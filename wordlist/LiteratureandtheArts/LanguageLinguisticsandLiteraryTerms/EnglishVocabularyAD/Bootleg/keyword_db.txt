boot·leg 
/ ˈboōtˌleg/
•
adj.
(esp. of liquor, computer software, or recordings) made, distributed, or sold illegally:
bootleg cassettes
bootleg whiskey.

•
v.
(-legged, -leg·ging) [tr.]
make, distribute, or sell (illicit goods, esp. liquor, computer software, or recordings) illegally:
[as  n.] (bootlegging)
domestic bootlegging was almost impossible to control.

•
n.
1.
an illegal musical recording, esp. one made at a concert.
2.
 Football
 a play in which the quarterback fakes a handoff and runs with the ball hidden next to his hip.
