[
cross·o·ver 
/ ˈkrôsˌōvər/
•
n.
1.
a point or place of crossing from one side to the other.
 ∎ 
a short length of track joining two adjacent railroad lines.

2.
the process of achieving success in a different field or style, esp. in popular music:
[as  adj.]
a jazz-classical crossover album.

3.
a person who votes for a candidate in a different political party than the one they usually support: [as adj.] crossover votes.

4.
[as  adj.]
relating to or denoting trials of medical treatment in which experimental subjects and control groups are exchanged after a set period:
a crossover study.
