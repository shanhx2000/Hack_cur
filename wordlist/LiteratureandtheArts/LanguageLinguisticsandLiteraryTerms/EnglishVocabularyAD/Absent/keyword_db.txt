[
ab·sent 
•
adj.
/ ˈabsənt/
1.
not present in a place or at an occasion:
absent from school
absent colleagues.
 ∎ 
(of a part or feature of the body) not forming part of a creature in which it might be expected:
wings are absent in certain flies.

2.
(of an expression or manner) showing that someone is not paying attention to what is being said or done:
she looked up with an absent smile.


•
v.
/ abˈsent/ (absent oneself)
stay or go away:
people absented themselves because of his presence
he absented himself from the table.

•
prep.
/ abˈsent/  formal
without:
employees could not be fired absent other evidence.

DERIVATIVES: 
ab·sent·ly
adv.
(in sense 2).
