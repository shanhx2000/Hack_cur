[
cad·die 
/ ˈkadē/
(also cad·dy)
•
n.
(pl.  -dies)
a person who carries a golfer's clubs and provides other assistance during a match.

•
v.
(cad·died, cad·dy·ing
) [intr.]
work as a caddie.

ORIGIN: mid 17th cent. (originally Scots): from French cadet. The original term denoted a gentleman who joined the army without a commission, intending to learn the profession and follow a military career, later coming to mean ‘odd-job man.’ The current sense dates from the late 18th cent.
