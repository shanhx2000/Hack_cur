co·in·ci·dent 
/ kōˈinsədənt; -ˌdent/
•
adj.
occurring together in space or time:
liberty is an idea coincident with the spread of Christianity. 
 ∎ 
in agreement or harmony:
the stake of defense attorneys is not always coincident with that of their clients.

co·in·ci·den·tal 
/ kōˌinsəˈdentl/
•
adj.
1.
resulting from a coincidence; done or happening by chance.
2.
happening or existing at the same time:
it's convenient that his plan is coincidental with the group's closure.
