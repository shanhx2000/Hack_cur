[
cluck 
/ klək/
•
n.
1.
the characteristic short, guttural sound made by a hen.
 ∎ 
a similar sound made by a person to express annoyance:
Loretta gave a cluck of impatience.

2. inf. 
a stupid or foolish person.


•
v.
(also cluck-cluck) [intr.]
(of a hen) make a short, guttural sound.
 ∎  [tr.]
(of a person) make such a sound with (one's tongue) to express concern or disapproval:
Michael clucked his tongue irritably.
 ∎  [intr.] (cluck over/at/about)
express fussy concern about:
they were cluck-clucking over the dishonor he brought to the office.
