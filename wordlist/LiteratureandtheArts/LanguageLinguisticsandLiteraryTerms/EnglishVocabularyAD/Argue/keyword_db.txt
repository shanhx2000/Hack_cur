[
ar·gue 
/ ˈärgyoō/
•
v.
(-gues
, -gued
, -gu·ing
)
1.
[tr.] give reasons or cite evidence in support of an idea, action, or theory, typically with the aim of persuading others to share one's view:
defense attorneys argue that the police lacked “probable cause” to arrest the driver.
 ∎  (argue someone into/out of)
persuade someone to do or not to do (something) by giving reasons:
I tried to argue him out of it.

2. [intr.]
exchange or express diverging or opposite views, typically in a heated or angry way:
don't argue with me.
[tr.]
she was too tired to argue the point.
