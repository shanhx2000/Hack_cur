[
au·then·tic 
/ ôˈ[unvoicedth]entik/
(abbr.: auth.)
•
adj.
1.
of undisputed origin; genuine:
the letter is now accepted as an authentic document.

 ∎ 
made or done in the traditional or original way, or in a way that faithfully resembles an original:
the restaurant serves authentic Italian meals
 ∎ 
based on facts; accurate or reliable:
an authentic depiction of the situation.
 ∎ 
(in existentialist philosophy) relating to or denoting an emotionally appropriate, significant, purposive, and responsible mode of human life.

2.
 Mus. 
(of a church mode) comprising the notes lying between the principal note or final and the note an octave higher. Compare with plagal.


DERIVATIVES: 
au·then·ti·cal·ly
/ -ik(ə)lē/ adv.
au·then·tic·i·ty
/ ˌô[unvoicedth]enˈtisitē/ n.
