[
cor·rec·tion 
/ kəˈrekshən/
•
n.
the action or process of correcting something:
I checked the typing for errors and sent it back for correction.
 ∎ 
a change that rectifies an error or inaccuracy.
 ∎ 
used to introduce an amended version of something one has just said:
after today—correction, she thought grimly, after tonight—she'd never see him again.
 ∎ 
a quantity adjusting a numerical result to allow for a departure from standard conditions.
 ∎ 
a temporary reversal in an overall trend of stock market prices, esp. a brief fall during an overall increase.
 ∎ 
punishment, esp. that of criminals in prison intended to rectify their behavior.
