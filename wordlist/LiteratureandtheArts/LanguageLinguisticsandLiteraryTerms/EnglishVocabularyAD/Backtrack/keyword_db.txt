back·track 
/ ˈbakˌtrak/
•
v.
[intr.]
retrace one's steps:
she had to bypass two closer farms and backtrack to them later |
fig. 
to backtrack a little, the case is a complex one. 
 ∎ fig. 
reverse one's previous action or opinion:
the unions have had to backtrack on their demands.
