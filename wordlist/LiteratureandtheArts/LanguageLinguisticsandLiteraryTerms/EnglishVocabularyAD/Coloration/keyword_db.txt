[
col·or·a·tion 
/ ˌkələˈrāshən/
•
n.
1.
a visual appearance with regard to color.
some bacterial structures take on a purple coloration.
 ∎ 
the natural color or variegated markings of animals or plants:
the red coloration of many maples.
 ∎ 
a scheme or method of applying color.

2.
a specified pervading character or tone of something:
the movement has taken on a fundamentalist coloration.
 ∎ 
a variety of musical or vocal expression:
a skillful singer can do much with coloration.
