[
clump 
/ kləmp/
•
n.
1.
a compacted mass or lump of something:
clumps of earth.
 ∎ 
a small, compact group of people.
 ∎ 
a small group of trees or plants growing closely together:
a clump of ferns.
 ∎ Physiol. 
an agglutinated mass of blood cells or bacteria, esp. as an indicator of the presence of an antibody to them.

2.
a thick extra sole on a boot or shoe.

3.
the sound of heavy footsteps.


•
v. [intr.]
1.
form into a clump or mass:
the particles tend to clump together.
2.
(also clomp)
walk with a heavy tread.
