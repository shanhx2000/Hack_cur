[
de·tox·i·fy 
/ dēˈtäksəˌfī/
•
v.
(-fies, -fied) [tr.]
remove toxic substances or qualities from:
the process uses chemical reagents to detoxify the oil.
 ∎ 
(usu. be detoxified)
treat (an alcoholic or drug addict) to remove the effects of drink or drugs in order to help them overcome addiction:
he was twice detoxified from heroin.
 ∎ 
[intr.]
abstain from drink and drugs until the bloodstream is free of toxins in order to overcome alcoholism or drug addiction.
 ∎  [intr.]
become free of poisonous substances or qualities:
you can help your body detoxify by cutting down on coffee.
