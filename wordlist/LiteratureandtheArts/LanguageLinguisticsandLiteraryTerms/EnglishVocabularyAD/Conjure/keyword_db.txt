[
con·jure 
•
v.
1.
/ ˈkänjər; ˈkən-/ [tr.]
make (something) appear unexpectedly or seemingly from nowhere as if by magic:
Anne conjured up a most delicious homemade stew.
 ∎ 
call (an image) to mind:
conjuring up the image of her mother's face.
 ∎ 
(of a word, sound, smell, etc.) cause someone to feel or think of (something):
one scent can conjure up a childhood summer.
 ∎ 
call upon (a spirit or ghost) to appear, by means of a magic ritual.

2.
/ kənˈjoŏr/ [tr.]  archaic 
implore (someone) to do something.
