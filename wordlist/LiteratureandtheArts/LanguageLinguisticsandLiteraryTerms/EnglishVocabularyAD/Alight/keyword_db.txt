[
a·light1 
/ əˈlīt/
•
v. [intr.]
(of a bird) descend from the air and settle:
a lovely blue swallow alighted on a branch.
 ∎ 
descend from a train, bus, or other form of transportation:
the conductor alights to push the cable car around.


PHRASAL VERBS: 
alight on
find by chance; notice:
her eyes alighted on the item.
a·light2 
•
adv. &amp; adj.
on fire; burning:
[as  adj.]
the house was alight when the firemen arrived |
[as  adv.]
flammable liquid was set alight. 
 ∎ 
shining brightly:
[as  adj.]
a lamp was alight |
fig. 
the boy's face was alight with excitement.
