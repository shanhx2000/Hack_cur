[
bris·tle 
/ ˈbrisəl/
•
n. (usu. bristles)
a short stiff hair, typically one of those on an animal's skin, a man's face, or a plant.
 ∎ 
a stiff animal hair, or a man-made substitute, used to make a brush:
a toothbrush with nylon bristles
the heads are made with natural bristle.


•
v. [intr.]
1.
(of hair or fur) stand upright away from the skin, esp. in anger or fear:
the hair on the back of his neck bristled.
 ∎ 
make one's hair or fur stand on end:
the cat bristled in annoyance.
 ∎ 
react angrily or defensively, as by drawing oneself up:
she bristled at his rudeness.

2. (bristle with)
be covered with or abundant in:
the roof bristled with antennas.
