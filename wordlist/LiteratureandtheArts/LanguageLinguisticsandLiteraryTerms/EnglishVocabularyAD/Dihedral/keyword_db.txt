[
di·he·dral 
/ dīˈhēdrəl/
•
adj.
having or contained by two plane faces:
a dihedral angle.

•
n.
an angle formed by two plane faces.
 ∎ 
 Aeron. 
inclination of an aircraft's wing from the horizontal, esp. upward away from the fuselage. Compare with anhedral.
 ∎ 
 Climbing 
a place where two planes of rock meet at an angle of between 60° and 120°.
