[
back·pack 
/ ˈbakˌpak/
•
n.
a bag, often supported by a metal frame, with shoulder straps that allow it to be carried on someone's back. 
 ∎ 
a knapsack used by students as a bookbag, or any small bag carried on the back.
 ∎ 
a load or piece of equipment carried on a person's back:
a two-tank scuba backpack.


•
v. [intr.] [usu. as  n.] (backpacking)
travel or hike carrying one's belongings in a backpack:
a week's backpacking in the Pyrenees
he has backpacked around the world.

DERIVATIVES: 
back·pack·er
