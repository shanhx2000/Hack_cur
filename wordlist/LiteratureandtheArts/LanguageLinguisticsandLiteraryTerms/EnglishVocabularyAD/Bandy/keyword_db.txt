[
ban·dy1 
/ ˈbandē/
•
adj.
(-di·er
, -di·est
)
(of a person's legs) curved so as to be wide apart at the knees.
 ∎  (often bandy-legged)
(of a person) having legs that are curved in such a way; bowlegged.


ban·dy2 
•
v.
(-dies, -died) [tr.] (usu. be bandied about/around)
pass on or discuss an idea or rumor in a casual way:
$40,000 is the figure that has been bandied about.
 ∎ 
exchange; pass back and forth:
they bandied words and laughs from one to another.


PHRASES: 
bandy words with
argue pointlessly or rudely:
don't bandy words with me, Sir!
