[
chum1 
/ chəm/  inf.,  dated 
•
n.
a close friend.
 ∎ 
a form of address expressing familiarity or friendliness:
it's your own fault, chum.


•
v.
(chummed
, chum·ming
) [intr.]
be friendly to or form a friendship with someone:
they started chumming around in high school.

DERIVATIVES: 
chum·mi·ly
/ ˈchəməlē/ adv.
chum·mi·ness
n.
chum·my
adj.
chum2 
•
n.
chopped fish, fish fluids, and other material thrown overboard as angling bait.
 ∎ 
refuse from fish, esp. that remaining after expressing oil.


•
v. [intr.]
use chum as bait when fishing.

chum3 
(also chum salmon)
•
n.
(pl.  same or chums
)
a large North Pacific salmon (Oncorhyncus keta) that is commercially important as a food fish.
