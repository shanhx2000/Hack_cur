[
be·lea·guer 
/ biˈlēgər/
•
v. [tr.] [usu. as  adj.] (beleaguered)
lay siege to:
he is leading a relief force to the aid of the beleaguered city.
 ∎ 
beset with difficulties:
the board is supporting the beleaguered director amid calls for his resignation.


ORIGIN: late 16th cent.: from Dutch belegeren ‘camp around,’ from be- ‘(all) around’ + leger ‘a camp.’
