[
damp·er 
/ ˈdampər/
•
n.
a person or thing that has a depressing, subduing, or inhibiting effect:
another damper on reactor development was the problem of safeguards.
 ∎ 
 Mus. 
a pad that silences a piano string.
 ∎ 
a device for reducing mechanical vibration, in particular a shock absorber on a motor vehicle.
 ∎ 
a movable metal plate in a flue or chimney, used to regulate the draft and so control the rate of combustion.


PHRASES: 
put a damper on
have a depressing, subduing, or inhibiting effect on:
he put a damper on her youthful excitement.
