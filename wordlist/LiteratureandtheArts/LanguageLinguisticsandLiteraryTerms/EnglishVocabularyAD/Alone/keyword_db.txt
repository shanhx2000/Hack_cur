[
a·lone 
/ əˈlōn/
•
adj. &amp; adv.
1.
having no one else present; on one's own:
she was alone that evening |
[as  adv.]
he lives alone. 
 ∎ 
without others' help or participation; single-handed:
team members are more effective than individuals working alone.
 ∎  [as  adj.]
isolated and lonely:
she was terribly alone and exposed.
 ∎ 
having no companions in a particular position or course of action:
they were not alone in dissenting from the advice.

2. [as  adv.]
indicating that something is confined to the specified subject or recipient:
we agreed to set up such a test for him alone.

 ∎ 
used to emphasize that only one factor out of several is being considered and that the whole is greater or more extreme:
there were fifteen churches in the town center alone.



PHRASES: 
go it alone inf. 
act by oneself without assistance. 
leave
(or let) someone/something alone
1.
abandon or desert someone or something.
2.
stop disturbing or interfering with someone or something.
