[
tam·bour 
/ ˈtamˌboŏr/
•
n.
1.
 hist. 
a small drum.
2.
something resembling a drum in shape or construction, in particular:
 ∎ 
a circular frame for holding fabric taut while it is being embroidered.
 ∎ 
 Archit. 
a wall of circular plan, such as one supporting a dome or surrounded by a colonnade.
 ∎ 
 Archit. 
each of a sequence of cylindrical stones forming the shaft of a column.
 ∎ 
a sloping buttress or projection in a court tennis or fives court.
 ∎ 
[usu. as  adj.]
a sliding flexible shutter or door on a piece of furniture, made of strips of wood attached to a backing of canvas:
a tambour door.



•
v. [tr.] [often as  adj.] (tamboured)
decorate or embroider on a tambour:
a tamboured waistcoat.
