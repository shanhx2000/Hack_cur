[
mon·o·lith 
/ ˈmänl-i[unvoicedth]/
•
n.
1.
a large single upright block of stone, esp. one shaped into or serving as a pillar or monument.
 ∎ 
a very large and characterless building:
the 72-story monolith overlooking the waterfront.
 ∎ 
a large block of concrete sunk in water, e.g., in the building of a dock.

2.
a large and impersonal political, corporate, or social structure regarded as intractably indivisible and uniform:
states struggling to break away from the Moscow-dominated communist monolith.
