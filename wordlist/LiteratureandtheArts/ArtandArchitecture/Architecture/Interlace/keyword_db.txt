in·ter·lace 
/ ˌintərˈlās/
•
v. [tr.]
bind intricately together; interweave:
the trees interlaced their branches so that only tiny patches of sky were visible.
 ∎  (interlace something with)
mingle or intersperse something with:
buttercups interlacing their gold with the silver of the daisies
discussion interlaced with esoteric mathematics.
 ∎  [intr.]
(of two or more things) cross each other intricately:
[as  adj.] (interlacing)
interlacing bundles of smooth muscle fibers.
 ∎ Electr. 
scan (a video image) in such a way that alternate lines form one sequence that is followed by the other lines in a second sequence:
[as  adj.] (interlaced)
interlaced displays.
