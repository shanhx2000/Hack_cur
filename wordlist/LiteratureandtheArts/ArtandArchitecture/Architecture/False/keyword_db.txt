false 
/ fôls/
•
adj.
1.
not according with truth or fact; incorrect:
 false results
the allegations were false.
 ∎ 
not according with rules or law:
false imprisonment.

2.
deliberately made or meant to deceive:
the trunk has a false bottom
a false passport.
 ∎ 
artificial:
false eyelashes.
 ∎ 
feigned:
a horribly false smile.


3.
illusory; not actually so:
sunscreens give users a false sense of security.

4.
treacherous; unfaithful:
a false lover.


DERIVATIVES: 
false·ly
adv.
false·ness
n.
fal·si·ty
/ ˈfôlsətē/ n.
