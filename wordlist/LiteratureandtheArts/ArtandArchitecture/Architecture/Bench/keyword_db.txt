bench 
/ bench/
•
n.
1.
a long seat for several people, typically made of wood or stone.
2.
a long, sturdy work table used by a carpenter, mechanic, scientist, or other worker.

3.
(the bench)
the office of judge or magistrate:
his appointment to the civil bench.
 ∎ 
a judge's seat in a court.
 ∎ 
judges or magistrates collectively:
rulings from the bench.


4. (the bench)
a seat on which sports coaches and players sit during a game when they are not playing.


•
v. [tr.]
withdraw (a sports player) from play; substitute:
the coach benched quarterback Randall Cunningham in favor of Jim McMahon.

PHRASES: 
on the bench
1.
appointed as or in the capacity of a judge or magistrate:
he retired after twenty-five years on the bench.
2.
acting as one of the possible substitutes in a sports contest.
