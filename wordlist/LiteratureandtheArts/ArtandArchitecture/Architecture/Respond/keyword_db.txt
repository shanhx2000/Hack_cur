[
re·spond 
/ riˈspänd/
•
v.
say something in reply:
[intr.]
she could not get Robert to respond to her words |
he responded that it would not be feasible |
[with  direct speech]
“It's not part of my job,” Belinda responded. 
 ∎ 
(of a congregation) say or sing the response in reply to a priest.
 ∎  [intr.]
(of a person) act or behave in reaction to someone or something:
she turned her head, responding to his grin with a smile.
 ∎ 
react quickly or positively to a stimulus or treatment:
his back injury has failed to respond to treatment.
 ∎ 
[tr.]  Bridge 
make (a bid) in answer to one's partner's preceding bid.


•
n.
1.
 Archit. 
a half-pillar or half-pier attached to a wall to support an arch, esp. at the end of an arcade.
2.
(in church use) a responsory; a response to a versicle.
