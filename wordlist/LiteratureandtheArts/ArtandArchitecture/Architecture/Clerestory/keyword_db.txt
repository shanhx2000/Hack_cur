[
clere·sto·ry 
/ ˈkli(ə)rˌstôrē/
(also clear·sto·ry)
•
n.
(pl.  -ries)
the upper part of the nave, choir, and transepts of a large church, containing a series of windows.
 ∎ 
such a series of windows in a church or similar windows in another building.
 ∎ 
a raised section of roof running down the center of a railroad car, with small windows or ventilators.
