[
rough·cast 
/ ˈrəfˌkast/
•
n.
plaster of lime, cement, and gravel, used on outside walls.

•
adj.
1.
(of a building or part of a building) coated with roughcast:
a plain stone building, roughcast and whitewashed.
2.
(of a person) lacking refinement:
she thought of the roughcast yeomen she would meet.


•
v. [tr.] (usu. be roughcast)
coat (a wall) with roughcast:
the walls were to have been roughcast at the entrance bay.
