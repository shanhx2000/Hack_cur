[
bil·let1 
/ ˈbilit/
•
n.
a place, usually a civilian's house or other nonmilitary facility, where soldiers are lodged temporarily.

•
v.
(-let·ed
, -let·ing
) [tr.] (often be billeted)
lodge (soldiers) in a particular place, esp. a civilian's house or other nonmilitary facility.

bil·let2 
•
n.
a thick piece of wood.
 ∎ 
a small bar of metal for further processing.
 ∎ 
 Archit. 
each of a series of short cylindrical pieces inserted at intervals in decorative hollow moldings.
