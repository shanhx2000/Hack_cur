chock  / chäk/
• n. 1. a wedge
or block placed against a wheel or rounded object, to prevent it from
moving.  ∎  a support on which a rounded structure, such
as a cask or the hull of a boat, may be placed to keep it steady.
 2.
a fitting with a gap at
the top, through which a rope or line is run.  
• v. [tr.]
(often be chocked)
prevent the forward movement of (a wheel or vehicle) with a
chock.  ∎  support (a boat, cask, etc.) on chocks.
