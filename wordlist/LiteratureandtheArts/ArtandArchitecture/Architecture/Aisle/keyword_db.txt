aisle 
/ īl/
•
n.
a passage between rows of seats in a building such as a theater, an airplane, or a train.

 ∎ 
a passage between shelves of goods in a supermarket or other building.
 ∎ 
 Archit. 
(in a church) a lower part parallel to and at the side of a nave, choir, or transept, from which it is divided by pillars.


DERIVATIVES: 
aisled
/ īld/ adj.
ORIGIN: late Middle English ele, ile, from Old French ele, from Latin ala ‘wing.’ The spelling change in the 17th cent. was due to confusion with isle and influenced by French aile ‘wing.’
