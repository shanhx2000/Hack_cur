[
pas·tiche 
/ paˈstēsh; pä-/
•
n.
an artistic work in a style that imitates that of another work, artist, or period:
the operetta is a pastiche of 18th century styles |
the songs amount to much more than blatant pastiche. 
 ∎ 
an artistic work consisting of a medley of pieces taken from various sources.
 ∎ 
a confused mixture or jumble:
his speech is a pastiche of false starts and unfinished sentences.


•
v. [tr.]
imitate the style of (an artist or work):
Gauguin took himself to a Pacific island and pastiched the primitive art he found there.
