burr 
/ bər/
•
n.
1.
[in  sing.]
a rough sounding of the sound r, esp. with a uvular trill (a "French r") as in certain Northern England accents.

 ∎ 
(loosely) a regional accent charcterized by such a trill:
a soft Scottish burr.

2.
(also bur)
a rough edge or ridge left on an object (esp. of metal) by the action of a tool or machine.

3.
(also bur)
a small rotary cutting tool with a shaped end, used chiefly in woodworking, medicine, and dentistry.

4. variant spelling of bur.
