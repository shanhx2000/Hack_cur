[
cres·cent 
/ ˈkresənt/
•
n.
1.
the curved sickle shape of the waxing or waning moon.
 ∎ 
a representation of such a shape used as an emblem of Islam or Turkey.
 ∎ 
(the Crescent)  chiefly hist. 
the political power of Islam or the Ottoman Empire.

2.
a thing that has the shape of a single curve, esp. one that is broad in the center and tapers to a point.


•
adj.
1.
in the shape of a crescent.
2. poetic/lit. 
growing, increasing, or developing.


DERIVATIVES: 
cres·cen·tic
/ krəˈsentik/ adj.
ORIGIN: late Middle English cressant, from Old French creissant, from Latin crescere ‘grow.’
