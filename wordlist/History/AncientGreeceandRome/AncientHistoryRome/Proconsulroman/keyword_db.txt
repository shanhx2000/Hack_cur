pro·con·sul 
/ prōˈkänsəl/
•
n.
1.
a governor of a province in ancient Rome, having much of the authority of a consul.
2.
a governor or deputy consul of a modern colony.


DERIVATIVES: 
pro·con·su·lar
/ -ˈkäns(y)ələr/ adj.
pro·con·su·late
/ -ˈkäns(y)ələt/ n.
pro·con·sul·ship
/ -ship/ n.

Pro·con·sul 
/ prōˈkänsəl/
•
n.
a fossil hominoid (genus Proconsul, family Pongidae) primate found in Lower Miocene deposits in East Africa, one of the last common ancestors of both humans and the great apes.
